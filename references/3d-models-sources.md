# 3D Models & Assets Sources (Curated for archviz-3d)

## Primary Free CC0 / Downloadable 3D (from 400-free-design-resources 3D Assets)
- Poly Pizza (https://poly.pizza) — Thousands of free low-poly 3D models. Ideal for quick viz.
- Quaternius (https://quaternius.com) — Free 3D model packs (CC0). High quality packs.
- Kenney Assets (https://kenney.nl/assets) — Classic free 2D & 3D game assets (CC0). Reliable for teaching.
- Poly Haven (https://polyhaven.com) — Free CC0 HDRIs, textures & 3D models. Production grade.
- Shapefest (https://www.shapefest.com) — 160,000+ free 3D shapes & objects.
- 3D Icons by Realvjy (https://3dicons.co) — Free open-source 3D icons.
- Sketchfab (free) (https://sketchfab.com/features/free-3d-models) — Huge library, downloadable free tier.
- Free3D (https://free3d.com)
- Superscene (https://superscene.pro) — Free 3D scenes & assets.
- Pixexid 3D / Threedee (https://threedee.icons8.com) — Free 3D illustrations.

## Tools for In-Browser Prototyping / Reference
- Spline (https://spline.design)
- Vectary (https://www.vectary.com)
- Womp (https://womp.com)
- Blender + BlenderKit (https://www.blender.org, https://www.blenderkit.com)

## Integration
- Download CC0 models to assets/models/ for template examples.
- Use HDRIs from Poly Haven for lighting in Three.js templates.
- Low-poly preferred for self-contained performance.
- Always note license in references.

**Gotchas** (Phase 3 focus):
- Model scale/orientation often needs manual fix in Three.js.
- High-poly kills browser performance — downsample or use low-poly proxies.
- Textures require proper loading in CDN self-contained setup.
